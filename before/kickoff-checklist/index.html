    Semester Kickoff Checklist¶

    A comprehensive checklist to ensure you're ready for the first day—and first week—of class. Use this as a reference each semester to make sure nothing falls through the cracks.

    Pro tip

    Bookmark this page! Many instructors return to this checklist semester after semester.

    2-4 Weeks Before Classes¶

    Syllabus & Course Materials¶

    • [ ] Update syllabus with current semester dates
    • [ ] Verify textbook information is current and available
    • [ ] Check that all linked resources (websites, articles) still work
    • [ ] Review and update office hours for the new semester
    • [ ] Update any policies based on last semester's lessons learned

    Canvas Setup¶

    • [ ] Import or create your Canvas course shell
    • [ ] Update the course home page with current information
    • [ ] Set up your module structure for at least the first 2-3 weeks
    • [ ] Configure assignment groups and grading scheme
    • [ ] Set up the Gradebook with your weighting preferences
    • [ ] Create or import your syllabus page
    • [ ] Test the student view to see what students will experience

    Logistics¶

    • [ ] Confirm classroom assignment and visit the room if unfamiliar
    • [ ] Test classroom technology (projector, microphone, document camera)
    • [ ] Request any additional technology needs from ITS
    • [ ] Review your roster for any students with ARS accommodations
    • [ ] Coordinate with TAs on responsibilities and meeting times

    Week Before Classes¶

    Canvas Final Prep¶

    • [ ] Publish your Canvas course (make it visible to students)
    • [ ] Schedule or send welcome announcement
    • [ ] Verify all first-week assignments are ready and published
    • [ ] Double-check due dates and availability windows

    Communication¶

    • [ ] Send welcome email to students (Canvas announcement or email)
    • [ ] Share any pre-class preparation expectations
    • [ ] Provide information about required materials/textbooks
    • [ ] Include how to reach you with questions

    Personal Preparation¶

    • [ ] Review your teaching notes for the first class
    • [ ] Prepare any icebreaker or first-day activities
    • [ ] Plan how you'll learn student names
    • [ ] Prepare a backup plan if technology fails

    First Day of Class¶

    Before Class¶

    • [ ] Arrive early to test technology
    • [ ] Write your name and course info on the board/screen
    • [ ] Have your slides/materials ready
    • [ ] Bring a backup of key materials (USB drive, printouts)

    During Class¶

    • [ ] Welcome students and introduce yourself
    • [ ] Review the syllabus highlights (not every word!)
    • [ ] Explain how to navigate Canvas and find resources
    • [ ] Share your communication preferences and response times
    • [ ] Give students something meaningful to do (not just "read the syllabus")
    • [ ] Start learning names—consider a name tent or brief introductions

    After Class¶

    • [ ] Note any students who were absent (for later follow-up if needed)
    • [ ] Send a follow-up announcement summarizing key points
    • [ ] Review and adjust plans for the next class based on what you learned

    First Week of Class¶

    • [ ] Post your first substantive content/assignment
    • [ ] Respond promptly to student questions to set expectations
    • [ ] Hold your first office hours (even if no one comes)
    • [ ] Check Canvas analytics to see who has and hasn't logged in
    • [ ] Follow up with students who haven't accessed the course
    • [ ] Touch base with your TAs about how the first week went
    • [ ] Make note of any adjustments needed for next time

    First Week Announcement Template¶

    Use this as a starting point for your welcome message:

    Welcome to [Course Name]!

I'm looking forward to working with you this semester. Here's what you 
need to know to get started:

**Before our first class:**
- Review the syllabus [link to syllabus page]
- Obtain the required textbook/materials [details]
- Complete the "Getting Started" module in Canvas [if applicable]

**Our first class:**
- Date, Time, Location
- Bring: [laptop, notebook, etc.]

**How to reach me:**
- Office hours: [days/times]
- Email: [your email]
- Response time: I typically respond within [24-48 hours]

Questions? Reply to this announcement or email me directly.

Looking forward to meeting you!
[Your name]
