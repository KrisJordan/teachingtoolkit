Zoom AI Companion is UNC's supported AI assistant inside Zoom. For instructors, the most practical features are **Meeting Summary**, which drafts notes after a hosted Zoom meeting, and **Meeting Questions**, which lets approved participants ask what has happened so far while the meeting is still in progress.

Use these features for course planning, logistics coordination, guest speaker debriefs, project meetings, and larger class sessions where participants have been notified. Avoid using them for advising, grading conversations, accommodation discussions, or other meetings where sensitive student information is likely to come up.

Treat Zoom AI Companion output as a draft. Summaries and answers can omit important context, misidentify speakers, or turn tentative conversation into firmer-sounding action items. Review and edit AI-generated content before sharing it with students, colleagues, or anyone outside the course team.

!!! info "Zoom AI Companion is off by default"

    [UNC enables access to Zoom AI Companion](https://its.unc.edu/2025/02/19/zoom-ai-companion-available/), but individual AI features are disabled by default. Sign in at [unc.zoom.us](https://unc.zoom.us), open **Settings**, then choose the **AI Companion** tab to decide which features you want available in meetings you host.

## Access for Instructors

### Review Your AI Companion Settings

1. Go to [unc.zoom.us](https://unc.zoom.us) and sign in with your ONYEN
2. Select **Settings**
3. Open the **AI Companion** tab
4. Review the settings for **Meeting Summary** and **Meeting Questions**
5. Choose whether each feature should start automatically or only when you start it during a meeting you host

Start conservatively. For most teaching meetings, a good default is to start AI Companion features manually and share summaries with **Only me (meeting host)** first so you can review and edit the summary before sending it to participants.

These settings apply only to meetings you host. When you attend someone else's Zoom meeting, the host's AI Companion settings and in-meeting choices determine whether AI Companion runs.

### Use It During a Meeting You Host

When a meeting is underway, the host or co-host can start or stop Meeting Summary from the in-meeting controls. Participants can request that AI Companion be started, but the host controls whether it runs.

If sensitive student information, personnel matters, health information, financial information, or other confidential content comes up unexpectedly, stop **Meeting Summary** from the Zoom toolbar. If sensitive content is captured before you stop it, redact that material before sharing the summary.

## Key Features for Instructors

### Draft Post-meeting Notes

The main summary feature is **Meeting Summary with AI Companion**. When the host enables it, Zoom uses speech-to-text data from the meeting to generate a summary after the meeting ends. Summaries can include key points, decisions, and action items, and they can be shared by email or through Zoom Chat when continuous meeting chat is enabled.

Use the draft as a starting point for your own follow-up. Check names, dates, decisions, and action items against your notes before sending the summary to students, a teaching team, or campus partners.

!!! success "60-second Idea: Cleaner Planning Meeting Follow-up"

    Use Meeting Summary for recurring planning or logistics meetings. Set sharing to **Only me**, review the action items after the meeting, correct names and deadlines, and then send the cleaned-up version to the group.

### Live Meeting Questions

Meeting Questions lets the host, or participants if you allow it, ask AI Companion questions about what has happened so far in the meeting. Zoom provides preset prompts such as "Catch me up," "Was my name mentioned?," and "What are the action items?," and users can also ask custom questions.

This can help someone catch up after joining late or stepping away without interrupting the discussion. The meeting host controls who can ask questions, including options such as only hosts, all participants, or participants only from the point when they joined.

Use the **from when they join** option when it would be inappropriate for participants to query discussion that happened before they entered the meeting.

## Teaching Use Cases

### Reduce Catch-up Interruptions in Larger Class Meetings

If appropriate for the meeting, enable Meeting Questions so participants can ask what has been covered so far. This can reduce interruptions from "Can someone catch me up?" messages while helping the class return to the shared conversation.

### Create Better Planning Follow-up

Use Meeting Summary for planning meetings, committee meetings, and course coordination. The draft summary can be especially helpful for extracting action items, but it should not become the official account of the meeting until you review it.

### Review Guest Speaker or Project Meetings

For meetings with many participants and clear notice, Meeting Summary can provide a quick draft of key topics and follow-up items. Review the draft before sharing it with students, guest speakers, project partners, or collaborators.

## What It Does Not Do

Zoom AI Companion does not:

- replace instructor review of meeting notes, decisions, or student-specific concerns
- reliably identify every speaker or capture every important point
- determine whether a meeting is appropriate for AI summary
- create an approved FERPA workflow outside Zoom
- remove sensitive content automatically before a summary is shared
- replace required captions, transcripts, or accessibility accommodations

## Data Privacy

Notify participants before enabling Zoom AI Companion. If AI Companion starts automatically, Zoom displays a notice as participants join; if you start it manually, tell participants verbally or in chat.

Zoom meetings that include identifiable student participation, questions, advising details, coursework, attendance, or performance information may involve FERPA-protected education records. AI-generated summaries can make that information easier to copy or forward, so keep summaries in appropriate UNC systems, share only what is needed, and remove student-identifying details when they are not necessary for the teaching purpose.

Like other meeting summaries, AI-generated summaries are University records and may be subject to public-records requirements. Handle them in accordance with UNC's [Records Management Policy](https://policies.unc.edu/TDClient/2833/Portal/KB/Article/132181/Records-Management-Policy), and review AI-generated summaries, action items, and AI Companion responses before sharing them.

Be especially careful with sensitive student, employee, donor, legal, health, or financial matters:

- Do not intentionally use Zoom AI Companion to create summaries or transcripts of sensitive conversations.
- Avoid enabling AI Companion for private 1:1 meetings or advising conversations where sensitive student issues may come up.
- If sensitive content comes up unexpectedly, stop **Meeting Summary** from the Zoom toolbar.
- If sensitive content is captured, redact it before sharing the summary.
- PHI may only be shared in meetings held in a HIPAA-protected Zoom account.
- PCI/payment card data and Social Security numbers should never be shared in Zoom.

Zoom does not use customer audio, video, chat, screen sharing, attachments, or similar customer content to train Zoom's or third-party AI models. Zoom AI Companion features still process meeting data to provide the requested AI feature, and some features may use third-party AI model providers.

## Cost and Access

Zoom AI Companion is available at no additional cost with UNC Zoom licenses. Instructors can manage Meeting Summary and Meeting Questions settings for meetings they host at [unc.zoom.us](https://unc.zoom.us).
