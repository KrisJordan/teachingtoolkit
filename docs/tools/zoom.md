Zoom AI Companion is UNC's supported AI assistant inside Zoom. For instructors, the most useful AI features are **post-meeting summaries** and **live questions during a meeting**.

These features are useful for course planning, logistics coordination, guest speaker debriefs, project meetings, and larger class meetings where participants have been notified. They are not a replacement for instructor judgment: Zoom summaries and responses can omit important context or introduce errors, so review and edit AI-generated content before sharing it.

!!! info "Zoom AI Companion is off by default"

    [UNC enables access to Zoom AI Companion](https://its.unc.edu/2025/02/19/zoom-ai-companion-available/), but individual AI features are disabled by default and must be enabled in your Zoom settings. Sign in at [unc.zoom.us](https://unc.zoom.us), open **Settings**, then choose the **AI Companion** tab.

## Getting Started

1. Go to [unc.zoom.us](https://unc.zoom.us) and sign in with your ONYEN
2. Select **Settings**
3. Open the **AI Companion** tab
4. Review the settings for **Meeting Summary** and **Meeting Questions**
5. Choose whether each feature should start automatically or only when you start it during a meeting

For most teaching meetings, a good default is to start features manually or to share summaries with **Only me (meeting host)** first. [UNC's Zoom AI Companion Overview](https://tdx.unc.edu/TDClient/33/Portal/KB/Article/392/Zoom-AI-Companion-Overview) specifically recommends this approach so you can review and edit the summary before sending it to participants.

[UNC ITS's Zoom AI Companion tips](https://its.unc.edu/2025/03/27/5-zoom-ai-companion-tips/) page walks through the campus-specific **Settings** > **AI Companion** path and explains that these settings apply to meetings you host.

## Key Features for Instructors

### Post-meeting AI Meeting Notes

Zoom calls this feature **Meeting Summary with AI Companion**. When the host enables it, Zoom uses speech-to-text data from the meeting to generate a summary after the meeting ends. Summaries can include key points, decisions, and action items, and they can be shared by email or through Zoom Chat when continuous meeting chat is enabled.

Only the host or co-host can start or stop Meeting Summary from the in-meeting controls. Participants can request that AI Companion be started, but the host controls whether it runs.

!!! success "60-second Idea: Cleaner Planning Meeting Follow-up"

    Use Meeting Summary for recurring planning or logistics meetings. Set sharing to **Only me**, review the action items after the meeting, correct names or deadlines, and then send the cleaned-up version to the team.

### Live Meeting Questions

Meeting Questions lets the host, or participants if you allow it, ask AI Companion questions about what has happened so far in the meeting. Zoom provides preset prompts such as "Catch me up," "Was my name mentioned?," and "What are the action items?," and users can also ask custom questions.

This can help someone catch up after joining late or stepping away without interrupting the discussion. The meeting host controls who can ask questions, including options such as only hosts, all participants, or participants only from the point when they joined.

Use the "from when they join" option when it would be inappropriate for participants to query discussion that happened before they entered the meeting.

## Teaching Use Cases

### Support Students Who Missed Part of a Class Discussion

If appropriate for the meeting, enable Meeting Questions and allow participants to ask what has been covered so far. This can reduce interruptions from "Can someone catch me up?" messages while still helping students rejoin the flow.

### Create Better Administrative Follow-up

Use Meeting Summary for planning meetings, committee meetings, and course coordination. The draft summary is especially helpful for extracting action items, but it should be treated as a draft until you review it.

### Review Guest Speaker or Project Meetings

For meetings with many participants and clear consent, Meeting Summary can provide a quick draft of key topics and follow-up items. Review the draft before sharing it with students or collaborators.

## Data Privacy and Records

[UNC's Zoom AI Companion Overview](https://tdx.unc.edu/TDClient/33/Portal/KB/Article/392/Zoom-AI-Companion-Overview) advises instructors to notify participants before enabling Zoom AI Companion. If AI Companion starts automatically, Zoom displays a notice as participants join; if you start it manually, tell participants verbally or in chat.

Like other meeting summaries, AI-generated summaries are University records and may be subject to public-records requirements. Handle them in accordance with UNC's [Records Management Policy](https://policies.unc.edu/TDClient/2833/Portal/KB/Article/132181/Records-Management-Policy), and review AI-generated summaries, action items, and AI Companion responses before sharing them.

Be especially careful with sensitive student, employee, donor, legal, health, or financial matters:

- Do not intentionally use Zoom AI Companion to create summaries or transcripts of sensitive conversations.
- Avoid enabling AI Companion for private 1:1 meetings or advising conversations where sensitive student issues may come up.
- If sensitive content comes up, stop **Meeting Summary** from the Zoom toolbar.
- If sensitive content is captured, redact it before sharing.
- PHI may only be shared in meetings held in a HIPAA-protected Zoom account.
- PCI/payment card data and Social Security numbers should never be shared in Zoom.

Zoom states that it does not use customer audio, video, chat, screen sharing, attachments, or similar customer content to train Zoom's or third-party AI models. Zoom AI Companion features do process meeting data to provide the requested AI feature, and Zoom notes that some features may use third-party AI model providers.
