    Canvas Announcements Checklist¶

    Effective announcements help students stay informed, reduce repetitive questions, and build a sense of course community. Use this checklist to craft announcements that students will actually read.

    The Anatomy of an Effective Announcement¶

    The TL;DR Principle

    Assume students will only read the first 3 lines. Put the most critical information there.

    Weekly Announcement Checklist¶

    Use this checklist when crafting your regular weekly announcements:

    Content¶

    • [ ] Lead with the most important information (TL;DR)
    • [ ] Include specific action items students need to take
    • [ ] List all due dates for the upcoming week
    • [ ] Mention any schedule changes or room changes
    • [ ] Preview what's coming in class this week
    • [ ] Link directly to assignments or resources mentioned

    Formatting¶

    • [ ] Use headers and bullet points for scannability
    • [ ] Bold key dates and deadlines
    • [ ] Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
    • [ ] Use emojis sparingly to draw attention to key items
    • [ ] Include a clear subject line that indicates the week/topic

    Timing¶

    • [ ] Post at a consistent day/time each week
    • [ ] Give students enough lead time to act on the information
    • [ ] Avoid posting late at night or early morning
