    Data Protection Basics for Instructors¶

    Instructors handle lots of different data every day, from your own lecture notes to students' final grades. When deciding what you can legally and safely enter into an AI tool or external software, UNC-Chapel Hill classifies data into "Tiers" based on sensitivity.

    Tier 0: Public Data¶

    Information that is already public or could be posted to a web site without any issues.

    Examples:

    • General academic concepts or public knowledge
    • Blank assignments (before students complete them)
    • Reading lists
    • A public course syllabus

    Tier 1: Internal / Business Data¶

    What it is: Internal drafts, notes, and regular operational teaching materials. It's not top secret, but you wouldn't necessarily want it published on the internet.

    Examples:

    • Rough drafts of a midterm exam
    • Personal lesson plans
    • Brainstorming notes for a new lecture topic

    Tier 2: Confidential (FERPA) Data¶

    What it is: Protected student records. This is the critical line for instructors. The moment a piece of information identifies a specific student and their academic performance or feedback, it is generally FERPA-protected.

    Examples:

    • Student grades
    • Identifiable student feedback or peer reviews
    • Roster IDs, ONYENs connected to performance
    • An essay written by a student (if connected to their identity)

    You MUST use officially protected tools. Currently, Copilot 365 is the only AI platform at UNC sanctioned for Tier 2/FERPA data. Do not upload graded papers, specific grade feedback, or identifiable student work to tools like PromptLab, MagicSchool, or personal ChatGPT.

    Tier 3: Restricted Data¶

    What it is: Highly sensitive information governed by stricter laws.

    Examples:

    • Social Security Numbers
    • Health or medical records (HIPAA)
    • Credit card numbers (PCI)

    No general-purpose AI tools are approved for Tier 3 data at UNC-Chapel Hill. If your work involves clinical data or restricted research, you will need specialized tools and should consult directly with UNC IT or the Library AI Studio.

    Rule of Thumb for Generative AI¶

    When looking at our tool guides, you'll see a quick reference to the Data Protection level. If an AI tool is free or consumer-grade, assume they might use your prompts to train their public models unless stated otherwise.

    • Tier 0 & 1 Only: Great for brainstorming, course design, rubrics, and drafting. Keep student names and grades out of it entirely.
    • Tier 2 (FERPA) Approved: Safe for analyzing student progress, generating feedback based on actual student work, or processing grades.

    For the official, technical definitions of these terms, see the UNC Information Classification Standard.
