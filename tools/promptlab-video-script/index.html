    PromptLab Demo Video Script¶

    Target length: ~3 minutes
    Audience: UNC faculty who may not have used PromptLab before
    Goal: Show what PromptLab is, why it matters for instructors, and demonstrate building a simple course agent

    INTRO (0:00–0:25)¶

    PromptLab is UNC's own AI chat platform. It's free, it's available to anyone with an ONYEN, and it gives you access to leading AI models from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and others — all in one place.

    What sets it apart from commercial tools like ChatGPT is that it's managed by UNC Libraries, your data isn't used to train AI models, and you can build custom AI agents and share them directly with your students.

    Let me show you how it works.

    SIGNING IN AND INTERFACE (0:25–0:50)¶

    Head to promptlab.lib.unc.edu and sign in with your ONYEN.

    (Screen: show the login page, click "Continue with ONYEN")

    Once you're in, this is the main interface. The chat window is in the center — it works like any AI chat you've used before. Up here in the top left, you can see which AI model you're chatting with, and you can click to switch models anytime. Over on the right side, you'll find controls for building agents, adjusting settings, or uploading files.

    CHATTING AND SWITCHING MODELS (0:50–1:20)¶

    Let's start with something simple. I'll ask it to brainstorm three active learning activities for an introductory biology lecture on cell division.

    (Screen: type the prompt, show the response)

    That's useful on its own. But now watch this — I'll click the model selector and switch to a different model, say Anthropic's Claude, and ask the same question.

    (Screen: switch model, re-send or start new chat with same prompt)

    You can even run two models side by side in the same conversation by clicking the plus button next to the model selector. This lets you compare responses and pick the best ideas from each.

    BUILDING A COURSE AGENT (1:20–2:20)¶

    Now here's the feature that I think is most valuable for instructors: custom agents.

    An agent is a custom AI assistant that follows your rules and knows your course materials. You build one by filling out a simple form — no coding involved.

    (Screen: click on Agent Builder in the right panel)

    I'll click "Create New Agent" and give it a name — let's call it "POLI 100 Study Partner."

    (Screen: type the name)

    For the instructions, I'll write something like: "You are a study partner for POLI 100 Introduction to American Politics. Help students review course concepts by asking questions rather than giving direct answers. Use the Socratic method. If a student asks about grades or deadlines, refer them to the syllabus or tell them to email the instructor."

    (Screen: type or paste the instructions)

    Now I'll upload my syllabus under File Context so the agent has it as background knowledge.

    (Screen: upload a PDF)

    I'll pick a model — ChatGPT 5 works well here — and click Create.

    (Screen: click Create)

    That's it. The agent is ready. Let me test it — I'll ask "What topics should I focus on for the midterm?"

    (Screen: show the agent responding using the syllabus context)

    It's pulling from the syllabus and guiding the student with questions rather than just handing out answers.

    SHARING WITH STUDENTS (2:20–2:45)¶

    To share this agent with your students, click the Share icon here in the Agent Builder. Search for students by ONYEN, give them Viewer access, and click Save. They'll find the agent in their Agent Marketplace the next time they log in.

    (Screen: show the share dialog, add a user, save)

    This means you control exactly how AI shows up in your course. You set the instructions, you choose the materials, and students get a consistent, structured experience.

    WRAP-UP (2:45–3:00)¶

    PromptLab is free, it's UNC-supported, and you can get started right now at promptlab.lib.unc.edu.

    One important note: PromptLab is approved for Tier 0 and Tier 1 data only. Don't enter student grades or other FERPA-protected information. For that, use Copilot 365.

    If you have questions, reach out to the Library AI Studio. Happy teaching.
