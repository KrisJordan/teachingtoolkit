    Gradescope¶

    Gradescope is a grading platform primarily designed for paper-based and PDF assignments that helps instructors grade more quickly and consistently. Its most valuable AI feature is answer grouping: the ability to group similar student responses together so you can make a grading decision once and apply it to every response in the group.

    For some question types, Gradescope's AI suggests groupings of student responses automatically. You still review the groups, decide on ambiguous cases, and decide how to score them. In the event of a grading mistake, Gradescope's "Regrade Request" feature streamlines students asking for a second look from instructional staff. This makes Gradescope especially useful for large courses, handwritten quizzes, problem sets with repeated answer patterns.

    Gradescope is not AI replacing grading judgment, it is AI reducing repetitive work.

    Gradescope's AI-assisted grading only helps cluster similar answers together. Instructors and TAs are responsible for creating the rubric and deciding scores.

    Getting Started¶

    UNC's integration with Gradescope simplifies adopting it in courses and ensures your enrolled students have direct access via Canvas.

    1. Associate Gradescope with your course via Canvas:
      1. Course Settings
      2. Navigation
      3. Find Gradescope 1.3, click the three dots, select "Enable"
    2. Navigate to Gradescope from within Canvas
      1. The first time you do this you will be asked to create a Gradescope course
      2. Subsequent visits will take you to the Gradescope dashboard
    3. See Gradescope's instructions for getting started.

    AI-Assisted Grading and Answer Groups¶

    What Answer Grouping Does¶

    Instead of grading 150 responses one at a time, Gradescope lets you group similar answers and score the whole group at once. This is especially effective when students tend to converge on a small number of common answers or common mistakes.

    When grading by group:

    • You see one sample submission for the group
    • You apply rubric items once
    • Comments apply to all students in the group
    • Gradescope moves you to the next ungraded group

    This reduces repetitive clicking and helps keep grading decisions consistent across the class.

    What the AI Actually Does¶

    For supported question types, Gradescope AI looks across student submissions and proposes groups of similar responses. You then review those groups and confirm/reject, merge, rename, or edit them before grading.

    Supported AI-assisted question types are:

    • Multiple choice using filled bubbles or checkboxes
    • Math fill-in-the-blank
    • Text fill-in-the-blank

    What It Does Not Do¶

    Gradescope does not:

    • grade essays or open-ended responses autonomously
    • replace the rubric or instructor judgment
    • work for every assignment type
    • remove the need to review individual submissions within suggested groups for errors or outliers

    The AI sorts, instructors and TAs assess.

    Why Instructors Find It Useful¶

    More Consistent Grading Across TAs¶

    Gradescope's grouped workflow and dynamic rubric help multiple graders stay aligned. When the course staff agrees on how one answer group should be scored, that scoring decision can be applied consistently across all matching responses.

    This is one of the main reasons large-enrollment courses adopt Gradescope.

    Faster Grading for Large Classes¶

    If 80 students arrive at the same wrong answer in a chemistry, economics, or calculus problem, you should not have to make the same grading decision 80 times. Answer grouping lets you apply the same rubric choice once and move on.

    Better Feedback on Repeated Mistakes¶

    Because comments can be attached to a whole group, instructors can give clearer feedback on common misconceptions without retyping the same explanation over and over.

    That often leads to more feedback, not less, because the repetitive part of the work is reduced.

    A Practical Workflow for Open Response¶

    Gradescope is useful even for question types, like hand written essays or code, that cannot be AI-graded. The user interface for reviewing individual responses and assigning rubric items is much more streamlined than alternatives.

    Best Uses for Teaching¶

    Learning Analytics¶

    After grading, Gradescope provides assignment statistics that help you see where students struggled.

    This matters because the grouped grading workflow often reveals patterns quickly:

    • Which wrong answer was most common
    • Which rubric item cost the most points
    • Which concept needs to be retaught next class

    That makes Gradescope useful not just for grading faster, but for adjusting instruction sooner.

    Data Privacy¶

    Gradescope handles student records and is FERPA compliant. It also states that it does not claim rights to your assignments or rubrics beyond what is necessary to provide the service, and that student records can be deleted upon request.

    Cost and Availability¶

    UNC has a site license for Gradescope's Institutional plan. Answer grouping and AI-assisted grading are enabled for all instructors at no additional cost.
