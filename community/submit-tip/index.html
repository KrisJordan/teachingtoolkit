    Share Your Teaching Tips¶

    Have a teaching tip, technique, or tool that's made a difference in your courses? We'd love to hear about it—and share it with colleagues across campus.

    What We're Looking For¶

    The best tips are:

    • Specific — Tell us exactly what you do, not just general principles
    • Actionable — Others should be able to try it themselves
    • Tested — You've used it and seen results
    • Honest — Including limitations or situations where it might not work

    Types of Contributions¶

    Quick Tips¶

    Short, actionable ideas that can be described in a few sentences.

    Example: "I start every class with a one-question quiz on the previous session's material. It only takes 3 minutes, but it dramatically improved retention and attendance."

    Detailed Practices¶

    Longer descriptions of teaching approaches with context and examples.

    Example: A full description of how you structure group work, including how you form groups, what roles you assign, and how you debrief.

    Tool Recommendations¶

    Technology or resources that have helped your teaching.

    Example: "I use [tool] for [purpose]. Here's why it works for me and tips for getting started."

    Faculty Spotlight Interview¶

    If you'd be willing to be interviewed about your teaching for a Faculty Spotlight, let us know!

    How to Submit¶

    Option 1: Quick Form¶

    Fill out our Google Form to submit a tip. (Takes about 5 minutes)

    Option 2: Email¶

    Send your tip directly to cfe@unc.edu with:

    • Your tip or practice
    • Your name and department (or note if you prefer to remain anonymous)
    • Any relevant context or examples

    Option 3: GitHub¶

    Tech-savvy? Submit a pull request directly to our GitHub repository.

    What Happens Next¶

    1. We review your submission for fit and clarity
    2. We might follow up with questions or requests for more detail
    3. We publish with appropriate attribution (or anonymously if you prefer)
    4. You help colleagues across campus!

    Attribution Options¶

    When you submit, let us know how you'd like to be credited:

    • Full attribution: Name, title, and department
    • Partial attribution: Name and department only
    • Anonymous: No identifying information
